Part II: Analysis of the experimental results

The analysis takes the given relevant results as the top results that are most desired. What we notice in the results is that the exact same number of results are retrieved in model 1 and model3. Both model 1 and model 3 did not use the stemming technique; in model 1 stopped words were removed and in model3 stopped words were included. This latter deviation may have changed the order of results but I don't believe this is the anomaly that we are interested in analyzing with this experiment. When we actually examine the result set we can see the flaws in adopting the technique of stemming if it is not implemented properly. In model 2, when we click on the top 50 documents list we see that the results are limited to the words, "user", "behaviour", or "user behavior" as a concatenated string. In the field of information retrieval, searching for stems has generally been deprecated in favor of algorithmically superior methods, such as searching for n-grams.
